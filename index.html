📖 How Waste Score is Calculated

Waste/5S Score Formula:

Waste Score = (DOWNTIME Awareness × 0.40) + (5S Self-Score × 0.60)

DOWNTIME Awareness (40%) — Each waste type you identify = 12.5%. Spotting all 8 = 100%.

5S Self-Score (60%) — Rate your workspace discipline honestly:

5S PillarAsk Yourself
SortDid I remove unnecessary items from my workspace?
Set in OrderIs everything in a designated, labeled place?
ShineIs my workspace clean and inspection-ready?
StandardizeAm I following the agreed-upon standards daily?
SustainAm I maintaining these habits without reminders?

DOWNTIME — 8 Wastes Guide:

D Defects — Errors, rework, corrections, failed handoffs
O Overproduction — Doing more than what's needed right now
W Waiting — Idle time waiting for approvals, info, or systems
N Non-utilized talent — Underused skills, people doing below their capability
T Transportation — Unnecessary movement of materials, files, or data
I Inventory — Excess stock, backlog, unread emails piling up
M Motion — Unnecessary physical/digital movement by people
E Extra-processing — Adding steps or detail beyond what's required

🔍 DOWNTIME Waste Spotter

Use these prompts to identify waste in your daily work

D

Defects

🤔 Ask yourself:

  • Did I have to redo or correct any work today?
  • Were there errors in data, reports, or deliverables?
  • Did I receive unclear or wrong information from someone?

💡 Tip: Keep a quick tally of how many times you fix something that should have been right the first time.

Examples: Typos in reports, wrong data in spreadsheets, rework after review, miscommunication causing redo
O

Overproduction

🤔 Ask yourself:

  • Did I create more than what was actually needed?
  • Did I send reports/updates nobody reads?
  • Am I attending meetings that don't need me?

💡 Tip: Before starting any task, ask "Who needs this and when?" If the answer is unclear, you might be overproducing.

Examples: CC'ing everyone on emails, creating 20-page reports when 2 pages suffice, pre-making materials "just in case"
W

Waiting

🤔 Ask yourself:

  • Was I idle at any point waiting for someone/something?
  • Did a slow system or approval delay my work?
  • Was I stuck because information wasn't available?

💡 Tip: Track wait times in 15-min blocks. You'll be surprised how much of your day is "waiting for X." This is usually the biggest waste for office workers.

Examples: Waiting for manager approval, slow software loading, queued for a shared resource, blocked by late deliverables from another team
N

Non-utilized Talent

🤔 Ask yourself:

  • Am I doing tasks that don't use my real skills?
  • Is there a skill I have that nobody knows about or uses?
  • Did I see a colleague doing something they're overqualified for?

💡 Tip: If you feel bored or under-challenged, that's a signal. If you're doing data entry when you could be analyzing data — that's wasted talent.

Examples: Engineer doing manual data copying, skilled worker stuck on admin tasks, ideas not solicited from frontline workers
T

Transportation

🤔 Ask yourself:

  • Did I move files/materials between systems unnecessarily?
  • Did handoffs between people cause delays or confusion?
  • Was physical material moved more than needed?

💡 Tip: In office work, "transportation" = moving information between systems. Copy-pasting between 4 spreadsheets? That's transport waste.

Examples: Exporting data to email to re-enter elsewhere, walking to a printer far away, multiple approvals across different tools
I

Inventory

🤔 Ask yourself:

  • Do I have a backlog of unread emails or unfinished tasks?
  • Are there materials, supplies, or files piling up unused?
  • Am I holding "work in progress" that hasn't moved in days?

💡 Tip: Your inbox count, unread Slack messages, and stale to-do items are all "inventory." High inventory = high stress and low flow.

Examples: 200+ unread emails, 15 open browser tabs from last week, half-done reports waiting for input, excess office supplies
M

Motion

🤔 Ask yourself:

  • Did I have to search for a file, tool, or piece of information?
  • Did I switch between too many apps/tabs to do one task?
  • Was my workspace set up so I had to reach/walk unnecessarily?

💡 Tip: Motion ≠ Transportation. Motion is YOUR movement. If you're clicking through 7 folders to find a file you use daily, that's motion waste. Put it on your desktop!

Examples: Searching for files, toggling between apps, walking to get printouts, scrolling through long lists to find data
E

Extra-processing

🤔 Ask yourself:

  • Did I add more detail or formatting than the customer/boss needs?
  • Am I following a step that adds no value but "we always do it"?
  • Did I over-engineer a solution when a simpler one would work?

💡 Tip: Ask "Would the customer/next person notice if I skipped this step?" If no — it's extra-processing. This is the sneakiest waste because it feels productive.

Examples: Color-coding a spreadsheet nobody prints, adding 10 approval steps when 2 suffice, writing detailed meeting notes no one reads

🎯 Daily practice: At end of day, spend 2 minutes checking which DOWNTIME wastes you encountered. Over time, you'll spot them in real-time!
